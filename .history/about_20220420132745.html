My About Page

I've had a fascination with programming since as long as I can remember. It initially began with video games. I have vivid memories of sitting in my basement and playing video games from sun up until sundown for days on end, always thinking one thing. "Man, I can't wait until I can make one of these myself someday." From a young age, I knew that video game development was what I wanted to do.

I started software development with Multimedia Fusion 2 when I was 10, and ever since that day I've been steadily picking up new languages, new concepts, and new skills. There's such a visceral thrill that I experience just by learning something new, and nowhere is that feeling stronger than learning a new programming concept. I've still retained my love for video games in all of its forms, and I would still love to develop one some day, but my admiration for programming has extended beyond that. My love for video games has long since evolved into a love of programming itself. Be it C++ or Java or Python, whether for apps or games or websites... no matter what it is, if it's programming, I am having a great time.

Aside from just programming, I have other interests in the STEM field. Throughout much of middle and high school, I participated in numerous FLL, FTC, and BEST robotics competitions, some of which we won awards at. I also aided in designing and building telemetry and task completion systems for the NASA 2018 Rover competition. I enjoy working both alone and in teams, which grants me a great deal of flexibility in projects. My first job working at Rosemeade Rainforest Aquatic Complex as a lifeguard helped foster both the confidence to act on my own judgement and the ability to plan and act alongside others at a moment's notice in time-sensitive environments. Furthermore, my love of learning allows me the capability to take on projects using skills I don't even possess yet (such as the aforementioned Rover competition). I pride myself on my versatility in capabilities when it comes to accomplishing a wide variety of tasks.

In terms of hobbies, I enjoy pretty much everything "nerdy". I love video games, especially RPGs and strategy games. I additionally collect video game consoles: everything from the Atari 2600 to the Nintendo Switch. I play Dungeons and Dragons and Starfinder in the enviable position of "forever DM". I frequently play card games, notably Magic the Gathering, as well as board games. When I can, I enjoy reading the occasionally Sci-Fi/Fantasy novel (although finding the time to do so gets harder every day.)
